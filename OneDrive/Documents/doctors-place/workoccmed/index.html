What We Do

Full-Service Occupational Health for Employers

From pre-employment physicals and DOT compliance to OSHA medical surveillance and return to duty evaluations — everything managed in one employer portal, available nationwide.

Pre-Employment Physical

Fitness-for-duty evaluations for new hires in any industry — construction, healthcare, manufacturing, transportation, and staffing. Confirm employees can safely perform their job before day one.

All industries · Non-DOT

DOT Physical Exam

FMCSA-certified medical examinations for CDL drivers. Covers all DOT agencies — FMCSA, FAA, FRA, FTA, PHMSA, USCG. Medical Examiner Certificate issued same day at 15,000+ sites nationwide.

$110 · FMCSA certified

Workplace Drug & Alcohol Testing

DOT-compliant and non-DOT urine, oral fluid, and hair follicle drug screens. Evidential breath alcohol testing (EBT). Pre-employment, random, post-accident, reasonable suspicion, and return to duty.

5, 10 & expanded panels

Random Drug Testing Program (C-TPA)

FMCSA-compliant consortium enrollment and random pool management for employers of all sizes. Quarterly draws, MRO review, Clearinghouse reporting, annual MIS reports, and audit-ready recordkeeping.

From $49/yr per driver

Return to Duty Physical

DOT return to duty testing after a drug or alcohol violation — negative test required before resuming safety-sensitive duties. Non-DOT fitness-for-duty clearance after injury or illness. Learn more →

DOT & non-DOT

Respirator Fit Testing

OSHA-compliant qualitative (QLFT) and quantitative (QNFT) respirator fit testing. Required for workers using tight-fitting respirators under OSHA 29 CFR 1910.134.

OSHA 29 CFR 1910.134

OSHA Medical Questionnaire

Physician-reviewed OSHA respiratory medical evaluation questionnaire (Appendix C). Required before any employee uses a tight-fitting respirator in the workplace.

Physician reviewed

Audiogram Testing

OSHA-compliant baseline and annual audiometric testing for workers exposed to occupational noise. Required under OSHA's Hearing Conservation Standard (29 CFR 1910.95).

OSHA 29 CFR 1910.95

Pulmonary Function Test (PFT)

Spirometry for workers exposed to respiratory hazards — silica, asbestos, chemical fumes. Required for OSHA medical surveillance programs in construction, mining, and manufacturing.

Spirometry · OSHA compliant

FMCSA Clearinghouse

Pre-employment and annual Clearinghouse queries, violation reporting, and driver consent management. Required for all DOT-regulated employers with CDL drivers.

FMCSA required

Vision & Color Testing

Snellen visual acuity and Ishihara color plate testing for pre-employment and annual occupational health screenings. Required for many safety-sensitive and transportation roles.

Pre-employment · Annual

Vaccinations & Titer Testing

Occupational vaccination programs — flu, Hep A & B, Tdap, MMR, varicella. Titer testing for immunity verification for healthcare workers, school employees, and new hires.

Healthcare · Schools · Construction

Common Questions from Employers

Most employers need at minimum: pre-employment physicals, workplace drug testing, and annual fitness-for-duty evaluations. DOT-regulated employers additionally need FMCSA-certified DOT physicals, random drug and alcohol testing programs, and Clearinghouse compliance. OSHA-regulated employers may also need respirator fit testing, audiograms, and pulmonary function tests depending on workplace hazards.
For DOT-regulated employees who violated drug or alcohol rules: the employee must complete a Substance Abuse Professional (SAP) evaluation, follow the prescribed treatment, then pass a return to duty drug or alcohol test before resuming safety-sensitive duties. Work OccMed provides the return to duty test at any of our 15,000+ nationwide collection sites. For non-DOT employees, return to duty is a fitness-for-duty evaluation by a physician confirming the employee can safely return after injury or illness.
