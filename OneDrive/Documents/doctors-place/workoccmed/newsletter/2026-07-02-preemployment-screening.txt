BUILD A SCREENING PROGRAM THAT PROTECTS EVERY HIRE
===============================================

Summer hiring is in full swing. Whether you're onboarding CDL drivers,
warehouse crews, healthcare staff, or a construction team, a complete
pre-employment screen keeps your people safe and your business compliant
from day one. Here's what a full program looks like — and a mid-year
checklist to keep it that way.

-----------------------------------------------
THE COMPLETE PRE-EMPLOYMENT SCREEN: one visit, everything a new hire needs
-----------------------------------------------

A strong program isn't just a drug test. Depending on the role and its
hazards, a pre-placement evaluation can bundle several services into a
single appointment:

  • Pre-Employment Physical — baseline fitness-for-duty exam
  • Drug & Alcohol Testing — DOT and non-DOT panels, breath alcohol
  • DOT Physical Exam — FMCSA med card for CDL roles
  • Respirator Fit Testing — QLFT & QNFT + OSHA questionnaire
  • Audiogram & PFT — hearing and pulmonary baselines
  • Vision & Color Testing — acuity and Ishihara screening
  • Vaccinations & Titers — Hep B, TB, MMR, flu and more

BEYOND THE HIRE: screening doesn't stop on day one
-----------------------------------------------

OSHA-driven medical surveillance is recurring. Workers exposed to noise,
respirable hazards, or requiring respirators need periodic audiograms,
PFTs, and fit-test renewals — typically annually.

And CDL fleets carry a quarterly obligation: Q3 started July 1. If you
run a random drug & alcohol program, it's time for a fresh selection.
2026 FMCSA minimums remain 50% for drugs and 10% for alcohol of covered
driver positions. Enrolled in our C-TPA consortium? Your Q3 draw runs
automatically.

-----------------------------------------------
MID-YEAR CHECKLIST: 6 things to verify before the auditor does
-----------------------------------------------

1. New-hire files — pre-placement exam + role-required screens on record.
2. Surveillance renewals — flag audiograms, PFTs, fit tests due in 60–90
   days and schedule now.
3. Medical certificates — catch DOT physicals / med cards expiring soon.
4. Random pool roster — add new hires, remove separated drivers.
5. Immunization records — confirm Hep B, TB, and required titers current.
6. Recordkeeping — results, chain-of-custody, and exposure records filed
   and retrievable.

TIP OF THE WEEK: Bundle role-based screens into one appointment. Sending a
warehouse hire for a physical one day and a fit test the next doubles
no-show risk and delays their start date. A single visit clears them
faster and cuts your admin time.
