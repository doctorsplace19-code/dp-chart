Build a Screening Program That Protects Every Hire

Summer hiring is in full swing. Whether you're onboarding CDL drivers, warehouse crews, healthcare staff, or a construction team, a complete pre-employment screen keeps your people safe and your business compliant from day one. Here's what a full program looks like — and a mid-year checklist to keep it that way.

 
The Complete Pre-Employment Screen

One visit, everything a new hire needs

A strong screening program isn't just a drug test. Depending on the role and its hazards, a pre-placement evaluation can bundle several services into a single appointment — so a candidate walks in unscreened and walks out cleared to start.

Pre-Employment Physical — baseline fitness-for-duty exam
Drug & Alcohol Testing — DOT and non-DOT panels, breath alcohol
DOT Physical Exam — FMCSA med card for CDL roles
Respirator Fit Testing — QLFT & QNFT + OSHA questionnaire
Audiogram & PFT — hearing and pulmonary baselines
Vision & Color Testing — acuity and Ishihara screening
Vaccinations & Titers — Hep B, TB, MMR, flu and more

Beyond the Hire

Screening doesn't stop on day one

OSHA-driven medical surveillance is recurring. Workers exposed to noise, respirable hazards, or requiring respirators need periodic audiograms, pulmonary function tests, and fit-test renewals — typically annually. And CDL fleets carry a quarter-by-quarter obligation:

Q3 started July 1. If you run a random drug & alcohol program, it's time for a fresh selection. 2026 FMCSA minimums remain 50% for drugs and 10% for alcohol of covered driver positions. Enrolled in our C-TPA consortium? Your Q3 draw runs automatically.
Mid-Year Checklist

6 things to verify before the auditor does

1.  New-hire files. Every summer hire has a completed pre-placement exam and any role-required screens on record.
2.  Surveillance renewals. Flag audiograms, PFTs, and fit tests coming due in the next 60–90 days and schedule now.
3.  Medical certificates. Catch DOT physicals / med cards expiring soon to avoid drivers going out of service.
4.  Random pool roster. Add new hires, remove separated drivers — a stale pool skews your selection math.
5.  Immunization records. Confirm Hep B, TB, and other required titers are current for healthcare and at-risk staff.
6.  Recordkeeping. Results, chain-of-custody forms, and exposure records filed and retrievable on request.
Tip of the week: Bundle role-based screens into one appointment. Sending a warehouse hire for a physical one day and a fit test the next doubles no-show risk and delays their start date. A single scheduled visit clears them faster and cuts your admin time.
