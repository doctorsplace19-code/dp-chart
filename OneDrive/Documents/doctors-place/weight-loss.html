FAQ

Common Questions

Am I a good candidate for semaglutide or tirzepatide?
GLP-1 medications are generally appropriate for adults with a BMI of 30 or higher, or 27+ with a weight-related health condition (diabetes, hypertension, high cholesterol). Our physician will review your health history during the consultation to confirm eligibility.
Do you accept insurance for weight loss visits?
We accept most major insurance plans for consultation and follow-up visits. Insurance coverage for GLP-1 medications varies by plan. We can help you check your coverage, and many patients use GoodRx or manufacturer savings programs to reduce medication costs.
How quickly will I see results?
Most patients notice reduced appetite within the first 1–2 weeks. Significant weight loss typically begins by weeks 4–8. Average weight loss is 15–22% of body weight over 6–12 months with consistent use and follow-up care.
Can New York patients get a prescription virtually?
Yes. NY patients can complete the full consultation, receive a prescription, and do all follow-up appointments via telehealth. The prescription is sent to your preferred pharmacy in New York.
What are the side effects?
The most common side effects are nausea, mild stomach discomfort, and reduced appetite — which is actually the desired effect. These typically improve after the first few weeks as your body adjusts to the medication. Our physicians monitor you closely and adjust dosing as needed.
