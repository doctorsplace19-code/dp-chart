Federal Requirements

Who needs a
consortium program?

Federal regulations under 49 CFR Part 382 require all employers with CDL drivers operating in interstate commerce to maintain a DOT-compliant random testing program.

49 CFR Part 382

Who Must Enroll

Any employer with one or more CDL-holding employees who operate commercial motor vehicles (CMVs) in interstate commerce is subject to FMCSA drug and alcohol testing rules. Small fleets that cannot meet minimum testing rates alone must join a consortium.

Annual Testing Minimums

Federal Random Testing Rates

Current minimum annual testing rates set by FMCSA under 49 CFR Part 382.305. Our program ensures your fleet meets or exceeds these requirements.

🧪 Drug Testing (Urine) 50% of drivers/year
💨 Alcohol Testing (EBT) 10% of drivers/year
Why join a consortium? Small fleets (often fewer than 10 drivers) cannot statistically meet the random minimum rates on their own. Pooling with other employers in our consortium ensures compliance year-round.
